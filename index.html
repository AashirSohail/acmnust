Who Are We ?

The world’s largest educational and scientific computing society, delivers resources that advance computing as a science and a profession. ACM serves its members and the computing profession with leading-edge publications, conferences, and career resources to get better experience out of their degree and develop new interests and passion for tech.

ACM's officially recognized chapter in National University of Science and Technology (NUST) located in Islamabad, Pakistan. We aim to provide a educational platform for our members to advance the field of Computing at NUST. We conduct workshops, seminars and various events to engage our members and provide a nourishing platform.

Event Timeline

Recent Events.


ACM DAY - 4 Sept 2018

NUST ACM Student Chapter kicked off its activities this year with ACM Day. The event was to let the freshmen know of the chapter and its activities. The membership drive started the same day and response was impressive.

ACM Recruitment Drive - 8-11 Sept 2018

With each passing day, ACM’s resolve to serve the community gets stronger and stronger. It continued to strengthen the resolve to reach as many individuals, as possible, to make them better professionals. With the fall semester coming to an end, and the spring in its full bloom, NUST ACM Student Chapter announced its recruitment drive for both technical and generic posts. It was an open invitation to anyone who wanted to help himself grow in the field of his particular interest. The Executive Member Drive constituted of a recruitment campaign for NUST ACM Student Chapter as well as ACM Women Chapter.

ACM Orientation Day - 18 Sept 2018

The orientation was conducted to welcome the new enterants in the Executive Council of ACM's student Chapter. Oath Taking Ceremony was planned to take whole executive body into confidence and to aware them of their responsibilities. Ice-Breaking Sessions were also conducted.

C/Python Workshop - 11-12 Oct 2018

Two day C and Python workshop was conducted to assist the students who were facing problems in the programming. The event went quite successful and turn out was great.

Visit to Educators - 1 Nov 2018

NUST-ACMW visited Educators Primary School to raise awareness among young girls about Computer Science and its applications. They were taught how to code from scratch.

Fundraising Campaign for Orphanage - 2 Nov 2018

NUST-ASWAN was able to raise Rs 20520 and arrange winter clothes for APNA Shelter.

Google Summer of Code - 5 Nov 2018

To spread the word about this opportunity, ACM conducted a seminar on Google Summer of Code(GSoC). With hardly any room to sit in the seminar hall(250+ attendees ), the event was largely successful. Two former participants of GSoC shared their experiences and encouraged more students from Pakistan to take part. The event ended with an active Q/A session.

MindFest 2018 - 12-15 Nov 2018

Four consecutive days of diverse activities with 400+ participants consisting of:

  • Ethical Hacking Seminar  
  • Speed Programming with Rs 2000 prize money
  • Poster Design Competition

GDG DevFest 2018 - 17 Nov 2018

NUST-ACM hosted DevFest in collaboration with GDG Islamabad. NUST-ACM helped GDG gain a funding of $900 from Github. The event was open for all people and 500+ individuals attended the event.

Introduction to Git/GitHub - 19-20 Nov 2018

A two-day workshop was held to introduce the Version Control System Git.

MLH Local Hackday - 1 Dec 2018


Registrations: 300 Participants: 125
Event was conducted in collaboration with Major League Hacking (MLH), hosted by GitHub with a funding of $300 and sponsored by Microsoft. From workshops about web development and coding projects to free food, it had it all. It was an open event.

Noreen Zindagi Trust Visit - 8 Dec 2018

NUST-ASWAN visited an orphanage and successfully conducted a game building workshop from scratch.

Telenor DigiGov Hackfest 2018 - 14-16 Dec 2018

NUST-ACM participated in the Hackfest organized by Telenor in collaboration with Saylani Welfare Trust, IGDA and Microsoft by volunteering as the organizing body and provided masters of ceremony. It was an open event.

Computer Science Education Week - 17-21 Dec 2018

Following NUST-ACM tradition a 5 –day event with 200+ consisting of different activities that were:

  1. Games
  2. Hour of Code
  3. Video Editing Workshop
  4. CERN Seminar
  5. Closing Ceremony

Peer to Peer Mentorship - 20-24 Feb 2019

Advisory sessions were given to students by upperclassmen to help with difficulties in their studies. Certificates were awarded to mentors who gave appropriate time allotted to their students.

Web Development Workshop - 13-14 Mar 2019

NUST-ACM W organized a two-day web development workshop to teach interested students HTML, CSS and NodeJs. A concession was given to female students to encourage their participation.

Seminar on Named Data Networking - 21 Mar 2019

Participants: 100+
Speaker: Hassan Ahmed (Assistant Professor at Georgia Southern University)
A seminar was held to highlight the current and future research challenges in NDN. It was particularly catered for seniors and grad students.

Cloud Study Jams - 28 Mar 2019

Study Jams are community-run study groups for developers. This event was conducted to help participants learn the fundamental tools and capabilities of Google Cloud.

How I met myself? - 2 April 2019

In this interactive seminar, with renowned speakers from Psychiatry and counselling backgrounds, NUST Character Building Society and NUST ACM Student Chapter tackled the root of Identity Crisis in a life-changing talk to help the eager minds of today to find their lost identity.

HackACM'19 - 27 Apr 2019

In partnership with GDG Islamabad, Techjuice, KNCT Hub, Grinphox and Developers Circle Islamabad, ACM-NUST shall be conducting its very own hackathon for students all over Pakistan. It is sponsored by GitHub, Byco.

Closing Ceremony- 3 5 2019

Which marked the end of 2019. The year was full of hardwork, fun memories and successful events that set the bar very high for other societies to meet. The council signed off hoping to be even better the next year.
